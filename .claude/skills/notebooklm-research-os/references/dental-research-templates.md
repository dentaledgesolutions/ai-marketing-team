# Dental Research Question Templates — SE Florida

Structured questions for each NotebookLM research workflow. Pass these to
`notebooklm ask` after sources are loaded. Adapt for the specific client,
service, and market.

---

## Market Research Questions

Ask in sequence after all sources are processed:

1. "What are the primary dental services patients in [city/neighborhood] are searching for or reviewing most frequently?"
2. "What patient concerns, fears, or objections appear most frequently across the review and FAQ content in these sources?"
3. "Who are the top 3-5 competitors in this market? How do they position themselves — by service, by price, by technology, or by relationship?"
4. "What content types or topics are competitors NOT covering that represent a gap this practice could fill?"
5. "What seasonal or cultural factors are specific to Southeast Florida that affect dental care demand or patient behavior in this market?"
6. "Based on these sources, what are the top 3 services this practice should prioritize in their social media marketing and why?"
7. "What patient language patterns — specific words, phrases, concerns — appear repeatedly? This will inform social copy and hook writing."
8. "Are there insurance, financing, or affordability signals in these sources that affect how this practice should position their services?"
9. "What trust signals (credentials, technology, community presence, languages) do leading competitors emphasize?"

Final: "Based on everything in these sources, write a market brief summary covering: competitive landscape, patient demand signals, top content opportunities, and 3 recommended campaign priorities."

## Competitor Analysis Questions

Ask after competitor sources are loaded:

1. "What services does [competitor name] actively promote on their website and in their content? Which services are most prominent?"
2. "What is [competitor]'s apparent positioning strategy — budget/value, mid-market, or premium? What language signals this?"
3. "What offers or CTAs does [competitor] use? Are they promotion-heavy or education-heavy?"
4. "What content themes appear in [competitor]'s public-facing material that are working for them?"
5. "Where does [competitor] appear weak or absent — which services, platforms, or patient segments are they not serving well?"
6. "How does [competitor]'s patient experience language compare to our client's? Where can our client differentiate on warmth, expertise, or community connection?"
7. "What languages does [competitor] serve? Is there a bilingual gap they are not filling well?"

Final: "Summarize [competitor]'s strengths, weaknesses, and the top 3 opportunities our client has to differentiate from them."

## Proposal Research Questions

Ask when building a research packet for a prospective client pitch:

1. "Based on [prospect name]'s website and available sources, what are their top 3 service priorities and how are they currently marketing them?"
2. "What is missing from [prospect]'s current digital marketing presence — platforms, content types, local angles, or bilingual content?"
3. "Who are [prospect]'s most direct local competitors and how are those competitors positioning themselves?"
4. "What patient demand signals exist in this market for [prospect]'s primary services?"
5. "What 3 quick wins could an AI marketing team deliver for this practice in the first 90 days?"
6. "What compliance risks exist in this practice's current marketing that an AI marketing team should address?"

## Clinical Topic Research Questions

For verifying accuracy before producing educational content:

1. "Based on the ADA and clinical sources provided, what is the current evidence-based guidance on [dental topic]?"
2. "What are the patient-facing explanations of [procedure] that are both accurate and free of alarming or misleading language?"
3. "What claims about [treatment] outcomes are supported by the evidence in these sources? Which claims would require caveats?"
4. "What are the most common patient misconceptions about [topic] that content should address?"
5. "Is there any safety or contraindication information about [topic] that marketing content must acknowledge?"

---

## Trend Deep-Dive Questions

For evaluating dental or lifestyle trends before committing them to the content calendar:

1. "What evidence in these sources supports or contradicts the claim that [trend] is gaining traction in SE Florida dental markets?"
2. "What dental services or patient concerns does [trend] most naturally connect to?"
3. "Are there any safety, compliance, or misinformation risks associated with [trend] that a dental practice should be aware of before participating?"
4. "What content formats and platforms are being used most successfully for [trend] content in healthcare or wellness?"
5. "Is there evidence that local SE Florida dental competitors are already using [trend]? If so, is there still whitespace?"
