# Dental Service Taxonomy

A structured reference of dental services for private practices. Used by all content skills to ensure accurate, patient-friendly language and correct service categorization.

---

## Service Categories

### 1. Preventive Care

**Goal:** Maintain oral health and prevent disease.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Prophylaxis | Teeth cleaning | "Start fresh," "maintain your healthy smile," routine care |
| Dental examination | Dental checkup / comprehensive exam | Peace of mind, catch problems early |
| Dental X-rays | Dental X-rays / digital X-rays | Safe, fast, thorough |
| Oral cancer screening | Oral cancer screening | Life-saving, quick, painless |
| Fluoride treatment | Fluoride treatment | Extra protection, especially for kids and high-risk patients |
| Sealants | Dental sealants | Protect kids' teeth from cavities |
| Periodontal maintenance | Gum health care / deep cleaning maintenance | Keep gums healthy after treatment |
| Nutritional counseling | Diet and oral health tips | Preventive education |

**Content guardrails:** Focus on routine care, accessibility, and ease. Avoid alarming language about disease or decay.

---

### 2. Restorative Dentistry

**Goal:** Repair or replace damaged or missing teeth.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Composite resin restoration | Tooth-colored filling | Natural-looking, durable, same-day |
| Amalgam restoration | Silver filling | Durable (less commonly promoted) |
| Dental crown | Dental crown / tooth cap | Protect and restore a damaged tooth |
| Dental bridge | Dental bridge | Replace a missing tooth without surgery |
| Dental implant | Dental implant | Permanent tooth replacement, closest to a natural tooth |
| Implant-supported denture | Implant-supported denture / snap-on denture | Secure, stable, comfortable |
| Full denture | Dentures | Replace all teeth |
| Partial denture | Partial denture | Replace several missing teeth |
| Root canal therapy | Root canal / root canal treatment | Save a natural tooth, relieve pain |
| Dental inlay | Dental inlay | Custom restoration for moderate damage |
| Dental onlay | Dental onlay / partial crown | Covers more than an inlay, less than a full crown |
| Full-mouth reconstruction | Full-mouth restoration | Rebuild your entire smile |

**Content guardrails:** Emphasize restoring function and confidence. Avoid graphic descriptions of damage or decay. Never imply a patient's existing work is substandard.

---

### 3. Cosmetic Dentistry

**Goal:** Improve the appearance of teeth and smile.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Porcelain veneers | Porcelain veneers | Transform your smile, cover imperfections, Hollywood smile |
| Composite veneers | Composite veneers / bonding veneers | More affordable, reversible |
| Dental bonding | Tooth bonding / cosmetic bonding | Fix chips and gaps, quick results |
| Teeth whitening (in-office) | Professional teeth whitening | Dramatic results, fast, safe |
| Teeth whitening (take-home) | Custom whitening trays | Whiten on your schedule |
| Smile makeover | Smile makeover / smile design | Comprehensive cosmetic transformation |
| Gum contouring | Gum reshaping / gum contouring | Frame your smile, address gummy smile |
| Tooth reshaping | Tooth reshaping / enameloplasty | Subtle, quick improvements |
| Lumineer | Lumineers | Thin, no-prep veneers |

**Content guardrails:** Focus on aspiration and confidence, not fixing "ugly" teeth. Never shame patients about their current smile. Before/after content requires HIPAA authorization.

---

### 4. Orthodontics

**Goal:** Straighten teeth and correct bite alignment.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Traditional braces | Metal braces / traditional braces | Proven, affordable, effective |
| Ceramic braces | Clear braces / ceramic braces | Less visible than metal |
| Lingual braces | Hidden braces / lingual braces | Completely hidden behind teeth |
| Clear aligner therapy | Clear aligners / Invisalign-style aligners | Removable, nearly invisible, discreet |
| Invisalign | Invisalign | Brand-specific — confirm practice is an Invisalign provider |
| Retainer | Retainer | Maintain results after orthodontic treatment |
| Space maintainer | Space maintainer | Hold space for permanent teeth (pediatric) |
| Palatal expander | Palate expander | Widen the jaw for better alignment |

**Content guardrails:** If promoting Invisalign specifically, confirm the practice is an authorized provider. Do not imply treatment is faster or easier than clinically realistic. Avoid before/after without authorization.

---

### 5. Oral Surgery

**Goal:** Surgical procedures involving the teeth, jaw, and oral structures.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Simple tooth extraction | Tooth removal / simple extraction | Quick, comfortable, gentle |
| Surgical extraction | Tooth removal / surgical extraction | For complex cases |
| Wisdom tooth removal | Wisdom teeth removal | Common, routine, relief from pain |
| Bone grafting | Bone graft / jawbone restoration | Prepare for implants, rebuild bone |
| Sinus lift | Sinus lift | Enable implants in the upper jaw |
| Frenectomy | Frenectomy / lip or tongue tie release | Improve function for children and adults |
| Implant placement | Dental implant surgery | First step toward a permanent tooth |
| Biopsy | Oral biopsy | Evaluate unusual tissue; tie to oral cancer screening |

**Content guardrails:** Emphasize comfort, gentle care, and expertise. Avoid graphic surgical descriptions. For pediatric procedures (frenectomy), emphasize relief and improved function for families.

---

### 6. Periodontics

**Goal:** Treat and manage gum disease and supporting bone structures.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Scaling and root planing | Deep cleaning / gum disease treatment | Address gum disease before it advances |
| Periodontal maintenance | Gum health maintenance | Keep gum disease in check with regular care |
| Gingivectomy | Gum reshaping / gum surgery | Treat severe gum disease or aesthetic issues |
| Osseous surgery | Gum surgery / bone reshaping | Reduce pocket depth, restore gum health |
| Gum graft | Gum graft / gum recession treatment | Protect exposed roots, restore gum line |
| Crown lengthening | Crown lengthening | Expose more tooth structure for a crown or cosmetic work |

**Content guardrails:** Gum disease content should be educational and non-alarming. Tie to systemic health connections (heart health, diabetes) carefully — do not make clinical causation claims without sources.

---

### 7. Pediatric Dentistry

**Goal:** Dental care for children from infancy through adolescence.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Pediatric exam | Kids' dental checkup | Fun, gentle, family-friendly |
| Pediatric cleaning | Kids' teeth cleaning | Build healthy habits early |
| Fluoride for kids | Kids' fluoride treatment | Extra protection for little teeth |
| Sealants for kids | Kids' dental sealants | Prevent cavities in back teeth |
| Pulpotomy | Baby root canal / nerve treatment | Save a baby tooth when needed |
| Stainless steel crown | Kids' dental crown | Durable protection for a damaged baby tooth |
| Space maintainer | Space maintainer | Hold space for permanent teeth |
| Pediatric sedation | Comfortable sedation dentistry for kids | Safe, gentle for anxious children |

**Content guardrails:** Parent-focused messaging. Emphasize comfort, friendly environment, and building positive dental habits. Avoid alarming parents unnecessarily.

---

### 8. Emergency Dentistry

**Goal:** Immediate care for dental pain, trauma, and urgent conditions.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency exam | Emergency dental visit | Same-day appointments, pain relief |
| Toothache treatment | Toothache relief | Fast relief, compassionate care |
| Cracked tooth treatment | Cracked tooth repair | Save the tooth, relieve pain |
| Knocked-out tooth | Knocked-out tooth emergency | Call us immediately — time matters |
| Lost filling or crown | Lost filling / crown replacement | Quick fix, back to normal fast |
| Dental abscess treatment | Tooth infection treatment | Urgent care, prevent spreading |
| Dental trauma | Dental injury care | Available when you need us most |

**Content guardrails:** Emergency content should be reassuring and action-oriented. Include clear CTA: call the practice or visit immediately. Avoid graphic descriptions of injury or infection.

---

### 9. Sedation Dentistry

**Goal:** Reduce anxiety and discomfort during dental procedures.

| Clinical term | Patient-facing term | Content angles |
|---|---|---|
| Nitrous oxide sedation | Laughing gas / nitrous oxide | Mild relaxation, wears off quickly |
| Oral conscious sedation | Oral sedation / relaxation dentistry | Take a pill, stay relaxed throughout |
| IV sedation | IV sedation / deep sedation | For complex procedures or high anxiety |
| General anesthesia | General anesthesia | Fully asleep; typically for oral surgery or pediatric cases |

**Content guardrails:** Target dental-anxious patients with empathetic, non-judgmental messaging. Never trivialize fear or imply patients are being dramatic. Safety messaging: always mention that a licensed professional administers sedation.
