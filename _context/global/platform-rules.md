# Platform Rules — Social Media Channels

Per-platform constraints, format specs, posting norms, and compliance notes for dental practice social media marketing in Southeast Florida. Read before producing content for any specific platform.

---

## Instagram

### Format specs

| Format | Dimensions | Max length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feed photo (square) | 1080 × 1080 px | — | Standard; safe zone for all feeds |
| Feed photo (portrait) | 1080 × 1350 px | — | Takes more screen space; higher engagement |
| Feed photo (landscape) | 1080 × 566 px | — | Lower engagement; use sparingly |
| Carousel | 1080 × 1080 px per slide | 10 slides max | High save/share rate; ideal for education |
| Reel | 1080 × 1920 px (9:16) | 90 seconds (sweet spot: 15–30s) | Hook in first 2 seconds; captions/subtitles required |
| Story | 1080 × 1920 px (9:16) | 60 seconds per clip | 24-hour lifespan; great for CTAs and polls |
| Story link sticker | — | — | Link to booking page, landing page, or lead magnet |

### Caption rules
- Maximum 2,200 characters; first 125 characters visible before "more"
- Lead with the hook — do not bury the point
- Hashtags: 3–10 relevant tags; include local (e.g., #MiamiDentist #BocaRatonDentistry) and service tags
- Emojis: optional; match brand voice; avoid medical emojis that could seem unprofessional
- CTA: include one clear action (book, DM, click link in bio, save)

### What works for dental practices on Instagram
- Before/after carousels (with authorization)
- Educational carousels ("5 signs you may need a root canal")
- Reel hooks that start with a surprising dental fact
- Team personality Reels (day in the life, office humor)
- High-quality smile photography (not stock photos)
- Patient testimonial Reels (with authorization)
- Instagram Stories with booking link sticker

### Compliance notes
- Paid promotions on Instagram are subject to Meta's health and wellness advertising policy
- Before/after content for ads may be restricted; verify in Meta Ads Manager before boosting
- Organic before/after posts are permitted but must have HIPAA authorization

## Facebook

### Format specs

| Format | Dimensions | Max length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feed photo | 1200 × 630 px | — | Landscape works better on Facebook than Instagram |
| Feed video | 1200 × 628 px or 1080 × 1920 px | 240 minutes (keep under 3 min for organic) | Auto-play without sound; captions essential |
| Reel | 1080 × 1920 px | 90 seconds | Shared across Facebook and Instagram if linked |
| Story | 1080 × 1920 px | 20 seconds per clip | Less commonly used on Facebook |
| Facebook ad image | 1200 × 628 px | — | Keep text under 20% of image for best delivery |
| Event cover | 1920 × 1005 px | — | For open house, community events |

### Caption rules
- No character limit effectively; longer-form posts can perform well with an engaged audience
- Facebook audiences (especially 35–65+) engage with more text than Instagram
- Hashtags: 1–3 max; Facebook hashtag discovery is weak compared to Instagram
- CTA: include a specific action ("Call us at XXX to book," "Click 'Book Now'")

### What works for dental practices on Facebook
- Educational long-form posts (gum health, what to expect from implants)
- Community event announcements and participation
- Patient milestone posts (with authorization)
- Facebook Reviews — respond to all reviews; never confirm clinical details
- Facebook ads with lead forms (book appointment, download guide)
- Year-end insurance reminder posts (high engagement with 40+ demographic)
- "Did you know?" posts

### Compliance notes
- Meta health and wellness advertising policy applies to all boosted and paid content
- Facebook ads for dental procedures may trigger additional review — allow extra time
- Review responses: never confirm PHI; keep responses generic and welcoming

## TikTok

### Format specs

| Format | Dimensions | Max length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok video | 1080 × 1920 px (9:16) | 10 minutes (sweet spot: 15–60s) | Hook must land in first 1–2 seconds |
| TikTok photo mode | 1080 × 1920 px | — | Swipeable; similar to Instagram carousel |

### Caption rules
- 2,200 character max; only first ~100 characters visible before truncation
- Hashtags: 3–5; mix trending dental tags, niche tags, local tags
- TikTok's algorithm is interest-based, not follower-based — local hashtags matter less than on Instagram
- Hook text on screen: first 1–2 seconds must grab attention; use on-screen text as a second hook layer

### What works for dental practices on TikTok
- "Things your dentist wishes you knew" style content
- Myth-busting ("This TikTok trend is actually destroying your teeth")
- Satisfying dental procedure close-ups (cleaning, whitening, polishing) — no PHI
- Day-in-the-life Reels from the dentist's perspective
- "Asking patients what they fear most about the dentist" (with consent)
- Trending audio applied to dental content (match the format, not just the sound)
- Educational explainers with strong on-screen text

### Compliance notes
- TikTok trends that involve DIY dental procedures (DIY veneers, DIY braces, DIY whitening with household products) are a hard veto — never participate or endorse
- Do not use any trending sound that has health misinformation or unsafe behavior as context
- Patient-facing video requires patient consent; do not record patients without explicit consent

## YouTube Shorts

### Format specs

| Format | Dimensions | Max length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| YouTube Short | 1080 × 1920 px (9:16) | 60 seconds | Discoverable via YouTube search; longer shelf life than TikTok/Reels |

### Caption rules
- Title: 100 characters max; include primary keyword ("What is a dental implant?")
- Description: include relevant keywords, practice name, city, phone number or booking link
- Tags: include service keywords and local city tags
- YouTube Shorts are searchable — SEO matters more here than on TikTok or Instagram

### What works for dental practices on YouTube Shorts
- FAQ format ("What does a root canal actually feel like?")
- Procedure explainers with clear, calm narration
- "Before you get [treatment], watch this" style hooks
- Patient education clips repurposed from longer videos

### Compliance notes
- YouTube Shorts are indexed by Google — accuracy matters more because content has a longer shelf life
- Claims that appear in Shorts may surface in search results; apply extra scrutiny to factual claims
- Avoid any claim that could be seen as medical advice rather than general education

## Google Business Profile (GBP)

### Format specs

| Format | Dimensions | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| GBP post image | 720 × 540 px minimum (1200 × 900 recommended) | Posts expire after 7 days (offers) or remain until deleted |
| GBP photo | 720 × 720 px minimum | Practice exterior, interior, team photos, equipment |
| GBP offer | — | Include redemption code and expiration date |

### Post types
- **What's new:** General updates, new services, new technology, team announcements
- **Offer:** Promotions with a clear expiration date
- **Event:** Open house, community health fair, continuing education event

### What works on GBP
- Regular posting (at least 1–2 posts per week) signals an active practice to Google
- New patient offers with a clear CTA
- Service spotlights with local keyword integration ("dental implants in Boca Raton")
- Seasonal content tied to patient behavior ("Use your dental benefits before year-end")
- Responding to every Google review — positive and negative

### Review response rules
- Respond to all reviews within 48 hours
- Never confirm the reviewer is a patient
- Never mention any clinical details in a response
- For negative reviews: acknowledge concern, invite them to call privately, do not argue or explain
- **Safe response template for positive reviews:** "Thank you so much for the kind words! We're so glad you had a great experience and look forward to seeing you again."
- **Safe response template for negative reviews:** "We're sorry to hear this wasn't the experience you expected. We take all feedback seriously. Please reach out to our office directly so we can make this right."

### Compliance notes
- GBP is not subject to HIPAA in the same way as direct communications, but responding to reviews with any patient-identifiable information is a HIPAA violation
- All GBP posts are indexed by Google — apply standard compliance review before posting

## Platform Priority by Practice Goal

| Goal | Primary platform | Secondary platform |
|---|---|---|
| New patient acquisition | Google Business Profile, Facebook | Instagram |
| Brand awareness | Instagram, TikTok | Facebook |
| High-revenue service demand | Facebook (ads), Instagram | YouTube Shorts |
| Community trust | Facebook, GBP | Instagram |
| Young adult patients (25–40) | Instagram, TikTok | YouTube Shorts |
| Older patients (45+) | Facebook, GBP | — |
| Hispanic/bilingual patients | Facebook, Instagram (Spanish content) | — |
| Emergency appointments | GBP (always-on) | Facebook |

## Cross-Platform Repurposing Guide

| Original format | Repurpose to |
|---|---|
| Instagram Reel | TikTok, YouTube Short, Facebook Reel |
| Instagram carousel | Facebook post (upload slides individually or as PDF) |
| Long-form Facebook post | GBP "What's new" post (abbreviated) |
| YouTube Short | Instagram Reel, TikTok, Facebook Reel |
| Blog post | Instagram carousel (5–7 key points), Facebook long-form, GBP post |

**Note:** Always review captions for platform-specific norms before repurposing. What works on TikTok does not always work on GBP.
