# Content Pillars

Six foundational content pillars for dental practice social media marketing in SE Florida. Every post belongs to one primary pillar. A healthy monthly content calendar balances all six.

---

## Pillar 1 — Education

**Objective:** Build authority, answer patient questions, reduce treatment fear, and improve oral health literacy.

**What it achieves:**
- Positions the practice as a trusted expert
- Attracts patients researching treatments before booking
- Reduces no-shows and cancellation anxiety by setting realistic expectations
- Performs well on SEO and saves-based algorithms (Instagram, Pinterest)

**Post types:**
- "Did you know?" facts about oral health
- Myth-busting ("You don't actually need to see a dentist every 6 months — here's the real answer")
- Explainer carousels ("What happens during a root canal — step by step")
- FAQ posts ("What's the difference between veneers and bonding?")
- Tips for at-home oral care
- Connection between oral health and systemic health (heart disease, diabetes — state as associations, not causation)

**Tone:** Informative, clear, jargon-free. Treat patients as intelligent adults, not patients who need to be lectured.

**Guardrails:**
- No clinical diagnosis framing ("If you have X symptom, you have Y condition")
- No fear-based urgency ("If you don't do this, your teeth will fall out")
- Cite or acknowledge that individual results vary
- Keep content accurate — do not simplify to the point of being misleading

**Recommended frequency:** 4–6 posts per month (20–30% of calendar)

---

## Pillar 2 — Trust and Social Proof

**Objective:** Build credibility through team personality, patient satisfaction signals, and community presence.

**What it achieves:**
- Converts researching prospects into booked patients
- Reduces anxiety for new patients who want to know what to expect
- Differentiates the practice from DSO competitors through human connection
- Drives review-based trust signals

**Post types:**
- Team introductions ("Meet Dr. [Name]")
- Behind-the-scenes office content (sterilization, new equipment, team lunch)
- Patient milestone celebrations (with authorization and no PHI)
- Practice milestones ("10 years serving Coral Gables")
- Awards, recognitions, CE courses, specializations (must be verifiable)
- Community involvement (sponsoring a school event, health fair participation)
- Response to Google/Yelp reviews (never confirm PHI in responses)
- Patient experience story (general, no identifying details) — requires authorization if specific

**Tone:** Warm, human, proud. Show the real people behind the practice.

**Guardrails:**
- Never identify a patient without written HIPAA marketing authorization
- Never fabricate awards, recognitions, or statistics
- Review responses must not confirm or add clinical details
- Before/after posts are trust content but require full compliance review and authorization

**Recommended frequency:** 3–5 posts per month (15–25% of calendar)

## Pillar 3 — Service Demand

**Objective:** Drive awareness and consideration for specific dental services and offers.

**What it achieves:**
- Moves patients from awareness to booking intent
- Surfaces treatments patients didn't know the practice offers
- Supports high-revenue service campaigns (implants, veneers, Invisalign)

**Post types:**
- Service spotlight ("Everything you need to know about dental implants")
- Offer announcement ("New patient special — exam + X-rays for $X")
- Treatment comparison ("Clear aligners vs. traditional braces — which is right for you?")
- Problem-solution framing ("Missing a tooth? Here are your options")
- Insurance and financing education ("We accept most PPO plans — here's how to use your benefits")
- "Now offering" announcements (new technology, new service, new provider)

**Tone:** Clear, confident, benefit-focused. Make it easy for patients to understand why this service matters to them.

**Guardrails:**
- All prices and offers must be verified in `approved-claims.md` before publishing
- Do not guarantee outcomes — use "may," "can," "many patients experience"
- Do not imply the patient needs a specific treatment
- Offers must comply with local advertising regulations and ADA guidelines

**Recommended frequency:** 4–6 posts per month (20–30% of calendar)

## Pillar 4 — Local Community

**Objective:** Establish the practice as a locally rooted, culturally connected member of the SE Florida community.

**What it achieves:**
- Differentiates from DSO chains through community identity
- Resonates with local patients who prefer to support independent businesses
- Increases geographic relevance in local search and social algorithms
- Builds relationships beyond the treatment chair

**Post types:**
- Local event participation or sponsorship
- Neighborhood or city shoutouts ("Proud to serve the Doral community")
- Seasonal/cultural moments relevant to SE Florida (Hispanic Heritage Month, local holidays)
- Local business cross-promotion (pharmacy, pediatrician referral partnership)
- School or sports team sponsorships
- Weather/lifestyle tie-ins ("South Florida sunshine is great — protect your smile all year")
- Bilingual community content for Spanish-speaking patients

**Tone:** Proud, warm, locally grounded. Use local references authentically, not performatively.

**Guardrails:**
- Avoid cultural content that could be seen as tokenizing or superficial
- Bilingual content must feel natural — not machine-translated
- Local statistics or references must be accurate

**Recommended frequency:** 2–4 posts per month (10–15% of calendar)

---

## Pillar 5 — Team Personality

**Objective:** Show the human side of the practice — who these people are, why they do what they do, and what working with them feels like.

**What it achieves:**
- Reduces dental anxiety by making the team feel familiar before the first visit
- Attracts patients who want a relational, not transactional, dental experience
- Supports hiring and staff retention through culture content
- High-performing organic content type on Instagram and TikTok

**Post types:**
- "A day in the life" Reels
- Team member spotlights ("Fun facts about our hygienist")
- Office humor and lighthearted moments (appropriate, professional)
- Doctor's personal story or philosophy of care
- Staff appreciation moments
- Celebrations (birthdays, work anniversaries)
- "Why I became a dentist" storytelling

**Tone:** Genuine, warm, slightly playful. The goal is to feel like a real person, not a brand.

**Guardrails:**
- Keep humor professional; avoid anything that could be interpreted as making fun of patients
- Do not share team member personal information beyond what they have consented to share
- Avoid politically charged content
- If team members appear on camera, confirm they have consented

**Recommended frequency:** 2–4 posts per month (10–15% of calendar)

---

## Pillar 6 — Promotional / Conversion

**Objective:** Drive direct appointment bookings, phone calls, form fills, and lead magnet downloads.

**What it achieves:**
- Converts engaged followers into patients
- Supports paid social campaigns with clear conversion creative
- Drives seasonal demand spikes

**Post types:**
- Limited-time new patient offers
- Year-end dental benefits reminders ("Use your insurance before December 31")
- Referral program announcements
- Lead magnet promotions ("Download our free guide to dental implants")
- Before/after campaigns (with full compliance review and authorization)
- Event-based promotions (back to school, holidays, tax season)
- Direct booking CTAs ("Book online in 2 minutes")

**Tone:** Clear, urgent (without being pushy), benefit-forward. Remove friction from the booking process.

**Guardrails:**
- All prices, offers, and terms must be verified and accurate
- Urgency must be genuine — avoid fake scarcity
- Before/after content requires HIPAA marketing authorization — never publish without it
- Conversion posts must pass compliance review before publishing

**Recommended frequency:** 2–4 posts per month (10–20% of calendar depending on active campaign)

---

## Monthly Pillar Balance Guide

A well-balanced 20-post month looks like this:

| Pillar | Posts | % |
|---|---|---|
| Education | 5 | 25% |
| Trust and Social Proof | 4 | 20% |
| Service Demand | 5 | 25% |
| Local Community | 2 | 10% |
| Team Personality | 2 | 10% |
| Promotional / Conversion | 2 | 10% |

**Adjust based on:**
- Active campaign (increase Promotional when a campaign is running)
- New service launch (increase Service Demand temporarily)
- Slow season (increase Trust and Team Personality to maintain engagement without hard selling)
- Client preference (some practices prefer less promotional content)
